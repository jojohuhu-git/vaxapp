🔗 Share & Open in Browser

📤 Way 1 — Share this session's data via URL

The URL below encodes all the age, dose history, and risk factors you entered. Anyone who opens this link sees the same patient state instantly.

How to open this URL in Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari:
  1. Click 📋 Copy URL above — the link is now on your clipboard.
  2. Open your web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari).
  3. Click the address bar — the long white bar at the very top of the browser that shows the current website address (it might say something like file:///C:/Downloads/vaccine-tool.html).
  4. Press Ctrl+A (Windows) or ⌘+A (Mac) to select everything in the address bar, then press Ctrl+V (Windows) or ⌘+V (Mac) to paste.
  5. Press Enter — the app opens with all patient data pre-filled.
  6. To share with a colleague: paste the URL into an email, Teams message, Slack, or text message and send it. They click the link to open it.
⚠️ The URL encodes vaccination dates and brands only — no patient names or MRNs are included. Use on HIPAA-compliant systems only.
💾 Way 2 — Open the downloaded file directly in Chrome (blank slate)

If you or your colleague downloaded vaccine-tool.html, here is how to open it:

  1. Find the file vaccine-tool.html in your Downloads folder (or wherever you saved it).
  2. Double-click the file — Chrome (or your default browser) opens it automatically as a local webpage. The address bar will show something like file:///C:/Users/YourName/Downloads/vaccine-tool.html
  3. The app is now fully functional — enter patient data and it works completely offline, no internet needed.
  4. To share the blank version: email the vaccine-tool.html file as an attachment. Your colleague saves and double-clicks it — it just works.
💡 Combining Ways 1 & 2: Open the file (Way 2), enter data, then use Way 1 to copy the URL and share it — your colleague opens the URL and sees everything already filled in.
🔖 Way 3 — Bookmark the app for quick re-access

After the app is open in Chrome: press Ctrl+D (Windows) or ⌘+D (Mac) to add a bookmark. Name it "PediVax" and click Save. You can then re-open it anytime from your bookmarks bar — no need to find the file again.
